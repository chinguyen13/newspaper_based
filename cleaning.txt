p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_A42A8206-721D-0D2D-6E96-F3F574C5DB16"
cite class="el-editorial-source">Seoul, South Korea (CNN)</cite>On his first trip to Asia as United States President last week, Joe Biden gave his strongest warning yet to Beijing that Washington was <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2022/05/23/politics/biden-taiwan-china-japan-intl-hnk/index.html" target="_blank">committed to defending Taiwan militarily</a> in the event of an attack from China.</p
/div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_84051064-FA29-B7C6-2010-F93360B5C6DB">Biden's comments, which compared a potential Chinese attack on Taiwan to <a href="https://www.cnn.com/specials/europe/ukraine" target="_blank">Russia's invasion of Ukraine</a>, appeared to deviate from Washington's decades-old policy of "strategic ambiguity" on the issue and seemingly raised the possibility of a military clash between US and Chinese forces.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_D5CD95BD-B495-30EA-2AD1-F937E6CBD975">It's the third time Biden has made similar remarks since taking office and, just as on the other two occasions, they were quickly walked back by the White House -- which insists its policy has not changed. However, it inevitably raises the question: if China tries to take Taiwan, are the United States and its allies able to stop it?</div

Analysts say China has more troops, more missiles and more ships than Taiwan or its possible supporters, like the US or Japan, could bring to a fight. That means that if China is absolutely determined to take the island it probably can.</div

US government documents put the number of killed, injured and missing from both sides during the almost three-month-long Normandy campaign at almost half a million troops.</div

div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_894B1AB3-80B9-5140-6C5C-FF6680486AB6">Taiwan's population of 24 million people is packed into dense urban areas like the capital Taipei, with an average of 9,575 people per square kilometer. Compare that to Mariupol, Ukraine -- devastated in the war with Russia -- and with an average of 2,690 people per square kilometer.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_B553F512-8A14-7799-5B94-F90F10E4521A">Despite its numerical advantages in sea-, air- and land-based forces in the region, China has Achilles heels in each arena of war that would force Beijing to think long and hard about whether an invasion is worth the overwhelming human cost.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_9C0B2D96-1F3A-4858-FFB6-F9A15392B8FC">Here are some scenarios of how a Chinese invasion might play out:   </div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_5BF2A132-D03B-508B-3939-F930DEBA7FED"
h3>The naval war</h3
/div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_3BCDBC29-4C76-3B25-2720-F9A1B825AA7A">China has <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2021/03/05/china/china-world-biggest-navy-intl-hnk-ml-dst/index.html" target="_blank">the world's largest navy</a>, with around 360 combat vessels --<strong> </strong>bigger than the US' fleet of just under 300 ships. </div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_5DC2F7C1-319E-5866-045B-F9A41E7A3790">Beijing also has the world's most-advanced merchant fleet, a large coast guard and, experts say, a <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2021/04/12/china/china-maritime-militia-explainer-intl-hnk-ml-dst/index.html" target="_blank">maritime militia</a> -- fishing boats unofficially aligned with the military -- giving it access to hundreds of additional vessels that could be used to transport the hundreds of thousands of troops that analysts say China would need for an amphibious invasion.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_76D749D2-88B5-0CC2-301E-FE50F4FB2EE0">And those troops would need massive amounts of supplies.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_49106BFD-A037-4B35-158F-FE50F627B01C">"For Beijing to have reasonable prospects of victory, the PLA (People's Liberation Army) would have to move thousands of tanks, artillery guns, armored personnel vehicles, and rocket launchers across with the troops. Mountains of equipment and lakes of fuel would have to cross with them," Ian Easton, a senior director at the Project 2049 Institute, <a href="https://thediplomat.com/2021/05/why-a-taiwan-invasion-would-look-nothing-like-d-day/" target="_blank">wrote in The Diplomat </a>last year.</div

div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_BAF4743F-34F4-6F90-794B-F9B483BE5B0F">Getting a force of that size across the 110 miles (177 kilometers) of the Taiwan Strait would be a long, dangerous mission during which those vessels carrying the troops and equipment would be sitting ducks.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_6EFB3030-9D9E-8242-C729-F9B21C7D4722">"The thought about China invading Taiwan, that's a massacre for the Chinese navy," said Phillips O'Brien, professor of strategic studies at the University of St. Andrews in Scotland.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_138AAA23-EFE8-FB82-DDAC-F9B6E56BE9DC">That's because Taiwan has been stocking up on cheap and effective land-based anti-ship missiles, similar to the Neptunes Ukraine used to sink the Russian cruiser Moskva in the Black Sea in April.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_72158C16-293A-AB81-35EB-F9A8C46F669F">"Taiwan is mass-producing these things. And they're small, it's not like (China) can take them all out," O'Brien said.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_B60FDE29-1A4C-A719-606A-F9A8C46F846E">"What's cheap is a surface-to-ship missile, what's expensive is a ship."</div

div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_B45C84D4-6630-AA9A-BF21-F9B94427EC34">Still, China could -- given its numerical advantage -- simply decide the losses were worth it, pointed out Thomas Shugart, a former US Navy submarine captain and now an analyst at the Center for a New American Security. </div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_50C86D50-C3D2-C422-093D-F9B93B74E477">"There's gonna be hundreds if not thousands of (Chinese) vessels there to soak up those (Taiwanese) missiles," Shugart said.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_40392E7C-81CD-4824-CE80-F9BBB657E73F">Missiles aside, China would face massive logistical hurdles in landing enough soldiers. Conventional military wisdom holds that an attacking force should outnumber defenders 3 to 1.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_84CE7E1D-44BA-72C6-50AE-F9BD683BBFDD">"With a potential defending force of 450,000 Taiwanese today ... China would need over 1.2 million soldiers (out of a total active force of more than 2 million) that would have to be transported in many thousands of ships," Howard Ullman, a former US Navy officer and professor at the US Naval War College, wrote in a <a href="https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/content-series/reality-check/reality-check-10-china-will-not-invade-taiwan/" target="_blank">February essay for the Atlantic Council</a>.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_46A83F3D-16EB-CF2A-1E33-F9BD683B1E56">He estimated such an operation would take weeks and that despite China's maritime strength, it "simply lacks the military capability and capacity to launch a full-scale amphibious invasion of Taiwan for the foreseeable future."</div

h3>Aircraft carrier killers</h3
/div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_5150AE3D-69D8-5D99-35B8-F9A8C46FE12F">Some of the problems that would face China's navy in Taiwan would also face any US naval force sent to defend the island.</div

div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_7B34C502-3944-37E4-C4BF-F9D394D3D6BD">The US Navy sees its aircraft carriers and amphibious assault ships, bristling with F-35 and F/A-18 jets, as its spear in the Pacific and would have a numerical advantage in this area. The US has 11 carriers in total, compared to China's two. However, only about half are combat ready at any one time and even these might be vulnerable.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_8F526ED7-E94B-F170-CB15-F9A8C46F3AD7">O'Brien and others point out that the People's Liberation Army has more than 2,000 conventionally armed missiles,<strong> </strong>many of which it has developed with the US Navy's prized aircraft carriers in mind.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_4DBDF4F3-C745-660B-A603-F9D13BA32174">Of particular concern would be China's DF-26 and DF-21D -- touted by Beijing's state-run Global Times tabloid in 2020 as <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2020/08/28/asia/china-missile-tests-exercises-intl-hnk-scli/index.html" target="_blank">"aircraft carrier killers"</a> and the "world's first ballistic missiles capable of targeting large and medium-sized vessels."</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_3F33C181-305D-4748-6903-F9A8C4707B8B">As O'Brien puts it, "The US better be careful thinking about, in any kind of war environment, sending carrier battle groups close to China ... If you're fighting a state-to-state war, you're going to stay far away from shore."</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_F9A3E03D-EC09-77AD-06F2-F9D2D0D37313">Others are more confident in the US carriers.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_5FC1D934-DB20-7F77-F771-F9A8C472BED2">Rear Adm. Jeffery Anderson, the commander of the US Navy's Carrier Strike Group Three centered on the carrier USS Abraham Lincoln, recently told CNN his ships are more than ready to deal with the kind of missiles that sank the Moskva.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_359FDA71-1C51-8A21-98CB-F9A8C4720CD4">"One thing I do know about our US ships is they're extremely survivable. Not only are they lethal, but they are extremely survivable," he said.</div

div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_CA363076-9C45-5EEA-706F-F9EC7D6C674E">Possible PLA targets could be Taiping Island, Taiwan's most far-flung outpost in the South China Sea; the tiny Pratas Island, a small outpost 170 nautical miles (320 kilometers) southeast of Hong Kong; Kinmen and Matsu islands, tiny territories just a few miles off mainland China's coast; or Penghu in the Taiwan Strait.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_3726FA47-F7A8-D851-F9E3-F9EC7D7271DA">While a PLA victory of any of the four is almost assured, it could come at the cost of galvanizing support for Taiwan in the rest of the world -- much as Russia's invasion of Ukraine has united the West against it.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_41E362AB-D618-17B6-4AC5-FA2C1F9FDCA8">Blackwill and Zelikow said the quarantine option might be more effective.</div
div class="zn-body__paragraph" data-paragraph-id="paragraph_E0DC31D9-28A5-A2AC-655E-F9EC7D7F6473">"In a quarantine scenario, the Chinese government would effectively take control of the air and sea borders of Taiwan," they wrote. "The Chinese government would run effectively a clearance operation offshore or in the air to screen incoming ships and aircraft. The screeners could then wave along what they regarded as innocent traffic."</div
